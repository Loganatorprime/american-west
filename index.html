The Era That Shaped America

From the Louisiana Purchase to the closing of the frontier, the American West was a land of opportunity, danger, and transformation. Cowboys, outlaws, Native Americans, and pioneers all played their part in one of history's greatest adventures.

Journey Through Time

Click on any event to learn more

1803

Louisiana Purchase

The United States doubles in size...

President Thomas Jefferson purchases 828,000 square miles of territory from France for $15 million, doubling the size of the young nation and opening the West for exploration and settlement.

Cost: $15 million Size: 828,000 sq mi
1804-1806

Lewis & Clark Expedition

The Corps of Discovery sets out...

Meriwether Lewis and William Clark lead the Corps of Discovery on a daring expedition to explore the newly acquired Louisiana Territory and find a route to the Pacific Ocean. Guided by Sacagawea, they traveled over 8,000 miles.

Distance: 8,000+ miles Duration: 2 years
1836

The Alamo

Remember the Alamo...

A pivotal battle in the Texas Revolution where approximately 200 Texan defenders held the Alamo mission against thousands of Mexican troops for 13 days. Though they were defeated, their sacrifice rallied the Texan cause.

Defenders: ~200 Duration: 13 days
1848

California Gold Rush

Gold discovered at Sutter's Mill...

James W. Marshall discovers gold at Sutter's Mill in Coloma, California, sparking one of the largest mass migrations in history. Over 300,000 people flocked to California, transforming the region forever.

Migrants: 300,000+ "Forty-Niners"
1862

Homestead Act

Free land for settlers...

President Lincoln signs the Homestead Act, offering 160 acres of public land to settlers who would farm it for five years. This act brought millions of settlers westward and transformed the Great Plains.

Land: 160 acres free Requirement: 5 years
1869

Transcontinental Railroad

East meets West at Promontory Summit...

The Central Pacific and Union Pacific railroads meet at Promontory Summit, Utah, completing the first transcontinental railroad. The "Golden Spike" ceremony marks a new era of western expansion and commerce.

Length: 1,912 miles Workers: 20,000+
1876

Battle of Little Bighorn

Custer's Last Stand...

Lieutenant Colonel George Custer and his 7th Cavalry are defeated by Lakota Sioux and Cheyenne warriors led by Sitting Bull and Crazy Horse. This battle marked the height of Native American resistance to westward expansion.

US Casualties: 268 Native Victory
1881

Gunfight at the O.K. Corral

The most famous shootout in Western history...

In Tombstone, Arizona, the Earp brothers and Doc Holliday face off against the Clanton-McLaury gang in a gunfight lasting only 30 seconds but becoming the most legendary moment in Old West folklore.

Duration: 30 seconds Shots fired: ~30
1883

Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show

The West comes to the world...

William F. "Buffalo Bill" Cody launches his Wild West show, featuring cowboys, Native Americans, sharpshooter Annie Oakley, and recreations of famous battles. The show toured America and Europe for 30 years.

Years Active: 30 Star: Annie Oakley
1890

Wounded Knee Massacre

The end of the Indian Wars...

US Army troops kill over 250 Lakota men, women, and children at Wounded Knee Creek, South Dakota. This tragedy marked the effective end of Native American armed resistance and the Indian Wars.

Lakota Dead: 250+ End of Indian Wars
1893

Frontier Declared Closed

The end of an era...

Historian Frederick Jackson Turner presents his "Frontier Thesis" at the Chicago World's Fair, declaring the American frontier officially closed. The era of westward expansion had come to an end.

Turner Thesis End of Frontier
